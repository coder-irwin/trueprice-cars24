# 06 · Responsible AI

TruePrice quietly influences what a person is paid for a major asset. That makes it a
high-stakes, consumer-facing AI system where **trust is the product**, not a feature. This doc
states our commitments and — crucially — points at where each one is *operationalized in code*,
not just asserted.

## 1. Honesty over optimism
**Commitment:** never show a tighter or higher range than the evidence supports.
**In code:** `pricing.py` derives the range from *quantile* models (not a cosmetic ± band) and
**widens** it when the variant is unresolved or condition is undisclosed. Less certainty →
demonstrably wider range and lower confidence. Enforced by
`tests/test_core.py::test_uncertainty_widens_range_and_lowers_confidence`.
**Guardrail:** we do *not* win by lowering every estimate to be "safe" — the goal is
calibration. Watched via the backtest's "avg final − floor" and the model card's coverage.

## 2. Explainability by default
**Commitment:** no black-box number. Every estimate says *why* and *what could change*.
**In code:** `pricing.explain()` produces a factor breakdown by **ablating the real model**
against a clean reference of the same variant (a transparent, model-derived attribution).
`condition.negative_disclosures()` produces the "what could change at inspection" list. Both
are rendered on the result screen. The breakdown reconciles with the point estimate within 3%
(`test_breakdown_reconciles_with_point`).

## 3. Expectation-setting, not bait
**Commitment:** the estimate is explicitly a *prediction*, and the inspection/auction step is
named — the exact "clearer communication" the CEO asked for.
**In code:** every estimate carries a `disclaimer` and `uncertainty_sources`; the UI leads with
"an estimate you can trust" and frames width as information, not error.

## 4. Fairness — price the car, not the person
**Commitment:** no feature proxies for the seller's identity, negotiating savvy, demographics,
or any protected attribute.
**In code:** the entire feature set (`model/features.py`) is *vehicle* attributes only — MSRP
anchor, age, km, owners, fuel, transmission, segment, condition, city tier, colour. There is no
seller feature to be unfair with. Fairness by construction, documented in `model/MODEL_CARD.md`.
**Note on `city_tier`:** it captures genuine demand geography (a real market effect), not the
seller — but it is the one feature to watch if geography ever correlates with protected groups
in real data; flagged here deliberately.

## 5. Data honesty
**Commitment:** be transparent about what the data is and isn't.
**In code/docs:** the dataset is **synthetic**, generated by `model/generate_data.py` from an
explicit, documented data-generating process — because real Cars24 data is proprietary and not
a free resource. Every number the model learns is reproducible from open code. We state plainly
(README, model card) that absolute rupee figures are *not* market-accurate; the deliverable is
the mechanics and the honesty properties.

## 6. Contestability
**Commitment:** a seller can see what drove the number and act on it — or dispute it with
context at inspection.
**In code:** the breakdown attributes each ₹ effect to a named factor ("Aftermarket CNG −₹X"),
so the seller understands *and can challenge* specific line items rather than a mysterious total.

## 7. Calibration & monitoring (how we'd keep it honest in production)
- **Coverage:** measured, not assumed — the model card reports the *actual* P10–P90 coverage
  (74%, honestly below the nominal 80%; conformal calibration is named future work).
- **Below-floor rate** is the north-star trust metric; the backtest is its offline proxy.
- **Distribution shift:** fuel-regulation changes, demand shocks, and new launches would move
  residual values → scheduled retraining, watched via `docs/04-metrics.md`.

## 8. Known limitations (stated, not buried)
- Synthetic data ≠ market truth; do not deploy the rupee figures.
- P10–P90 band slightly under-covers nominal (calibration is future work).
- Catalog covers 8 models; the resolver only disambiguates within a known model.
- No photo/RTO evidence yet — the resolver relies on honest self-reported features (mitigated
  by soft likelihoods and by widening under low confidence).

## Summary
Every principle above maps to a concrete mechanism and, where possible, a test. The through-line
is the same as the product's: **we tell the seller the truth, including the truth about how sure
we are.**
