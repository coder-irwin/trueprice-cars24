# Model Card — TruePrice Quantile Pricing Model

A model card in the spirit of Mitchell et al. (2019). Kept honest, because honesty is the
product (see `docs/06-responsible-ai.md`).

## Overview
- **Purpose:** predict the realized auction **final price** of a used car, as a *range*
  (P10 / P50 / P90), so a seller sees an honest, uncertainty-aware estimate instead of a
  falsely-precise number.
- **Type:** three `HistGradientBoostingRegressor` models (scikit-learn) trained with
  **quantile loss** at α = 0.10, 0.50, 0.90. P50 is the point estimate; P10–P90 is the range.
- **Owner:** TruePrice (this project).
- **License / cost:** 100% free & open-source stack (scikit-learn, numpy, pandas).

## Intended use
- **In scope:** produce a seller-facing estimate range + a factor breakdown, for the 8
  catalog models. Feed a confidence-scored lead to inspection/auction.
- **Out of scope:** a final/binding offer (that comes from inspection + live auction);
  models outside the catalog; insurance/loan valuation; anything treated as ground truth.

## Data
- **Source:** **synthetic**, generated by `model/generate_data.py` from an explicit,
  documented data-generating process (DGP). We do **not** use Cars24 proprietary data
  (not free, not ours). Every number the model learns is reproducible from open code.
- **Size:** 24,000 rows; 80/20 train/test split (seeded).
- **Why synthetic is acceptable here:** the goal of this MVP is to demonstrate the
  *product mechanics and the honesty properties*, not to ship production prices. The DGP
  is grounded in public market knowledge (depreciation curves, condition penalties). See
  `docs/00-research.md` §3.

## Features
Numeric: `ref_price_new` (variant MSRP anchor from catalog), `age`, `km`, `owners`.
Categorical: `fuel`, `transmission`, `segment`, `accident`, `aftermarket_cng`,
`service_records`, `tyres`, `insurance`, `city_tier`, `color`.
Defined once in `model/features.py` and shared by trainer and server (no drift).

## Evaluation (held-out 20% test, actual run)
| Metric | Value | Target | Reading |
|---|---|---|---|
| Point (P50) MAPE | **4.3%** | lower | point estimate is accurate on the DGP |
| P10–P90 coverage | **74.2%** | ≈ 80% | slightly narrow vs nominal — reported honestly |
| Avg range width | **12.9%** of point | — | tight because inputs here are *known-true* |

**On coverage:** GBM quantile bands commonly under-cover their nominal level; we report the
*measured* 74% rather than the nominal 80%. A production system would calibrate this
(e.g. conformal prediction) — noted as known future work, not hidden.

**Crucial nuance:** this width (~13%) is the uncertainty when the variant and condition are
*already correct*. In real life they often aren't — which is exactly the CEO's point. So at
serve time TruePrice **widens the range further** when the Variant Resolver is unsure or
condition is undisclosed (see `backend/app/pricing.py` → confidence scoring). The model gives
the data-driven core; the product layer adds honesty about *input* uncertainty.

## Ethical considerations / limitations
- **No personal or protected attributes.** Price is a function of the car, never the seller.
  There is no feature that proxies for who the seller is, their negotiating skill, or any
  protected class. Fairness by construction.
- **Synthetic data ≠ market truth.** Real depreciation, regional demand, and model-specific
  residual-value quirks are richer than the DGP. Do not read the absolute rupee figures as
  market-accurate; read the *mechanics and calibration behaviour* as the deliverable.
- **Distribution shift.** Fuel-regulation changes (e.g. diesel de-registration age limits),
  demand shocks, and new-model launches would shift residual values; the model would need
  retraining. Monitored via the metrics in `docs/04-metrics.md`.
- **Not a binding quote.** Always labelled a prediction; the inspection/auction step is named.

## Reproduce
```bash
.venv/bin/python model/generate_data.py --rows 24000   # rebuild data
.venv/bin/python model/train.py                        # retrain + print metrics
```
